"In the heart of a narrow cobblestone street stood an ancient clock shop, its windows clouded with dust and time. Inside, the clockmaker, a man with silver hair and eyes like polished brass, worked in silence. Each tick and tock seemed to echo his heartbeat, steady and deliberate. Few knew that every clock he crafted held a fragment of a memory—his own or one entrusted to him by strangers. When the hands of a certain golden pocket watch struck midnight, it whispered the laughter of a lost love into the still air. The clockmaker would pause, close his eyes, and let the sound carry him back to a summer long gone, knowing that some moments, once captured, could keep time from truly moving forward."
